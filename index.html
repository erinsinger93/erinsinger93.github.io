ABOUT
I am a junior at Stanford University studying Computer Science with a concentration in Human-Computer Interaction. Maybe it all began with building elaborate sand castles on the beaches of San Diego close to where I grew up, but I've always loved to create & design.

Before falling in love with Computer Science, I studied Neuroscience and Psychology and learned to appreciate how our brains help us perceive the world- including design. This knowledge now plays an integral role in my design process.

When I'm not programming and designing, I like to play retro videogames, watch Marvel movies, play softball, and listen to 80's music.

EXPERIENCE

TUTERA   |   SEPT 2014 - PRESENT
Tutera is a mobile-optimized webapp that aims to radically simplify the tutoring experience by streamlining the process from finding and meeting with a tutor to dealing with payment and feedback. I am the primary designer and front-end developer for Tutera. Though Tutera was originally designed for a Stanford course on Human-Computer Interaction, my team of four went above and beyond to almost fully implement an interface that is complete with a back-end. Screenshots of all pages I helped design can be found HERE.

COOLIRIS   |   APRIL 2014 - SEPT 2014
Recently acquired by Yahoo, Cooliris develops photo viewing products for both mobile and web platforms. When I began working for Cooliris mid-sophomore year, Cooliris was in the process of creating the app BeamIt. BeamIt is a photo messaging app that allows users to send up to thousands of high resolution photos at one time in private or group messages. I helped style the web application of BeamIt (now in beta) and helped build the website promoting the IOS application of Beamit. Screenshots of some of these contributions can be found HERE.
